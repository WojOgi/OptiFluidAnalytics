Analytical Capabilities

Thermal Dynamics & Atmosphere

Precise characterization over an extreme temperature range (-50 °C to 560 °C) under controlled atmospheres including vacuum, dry N₂ flow, or experimental air. Perfect for detecting thermal degradation onset and kinetics, glass transition temperatures (Tg), residual mass, solid-state reactions, phase transitions, and dewetting.

Fluid & Vapor Interactions

Real-time monitoring of material responses during immersion in complex liquids (organics, acids, bases) or exposure to controlled vapors, including binary mixtures. Ideal for tracking swelling magnitude and kinetics, sorption capacity, dissolution rates, and morphological stability near room temperature.

Thickness & Optical Constants

Determine absolute film thickness (sub-nanometer to microns) and optical constants (n, k) across a wide spectral range and on various substrates. We utilize advanced optical modeling techniques for complex multi-layer dielectric and absorbing film stacks, porous architectures, and composite materials.

High-Pressure Gas Roadmap

Upcoming capability: We are actively developing setups for in-situ ellipsometry under high-pressure gas streams (e.g., CO₂, CH₄, N₂, H₂ up to 50 bar). This will enable direct observation of high-pressure gas sorption, material stability, or penetrant-induced plasticization at industrially-relevant conditions.
